Regulatory Case Study Series

Qatar Pharma v. Kingdom of Saudi Arabia

Enforcement of Arbitral Award & Investment Treaty Breaches

Case No. 1:25-cv-07580 (SDNY)
Date: December 13, 2025
Status: Fully Briefed

Case Overview

This case study examines the petition by Qatar Pharma and Dr. Al Sulaiti to confirm a $100 million ICC arbitral award against Saudi Arabia. The dispute arises from the 2017 diplomatic blockade, which the tribunal found breached the OIC Investment Agreement.

The claimants assert that the blockade repudiated long-term supply contracts, forced the closure of warehouses, and destroyed the value of their investments in the Kingdom. The case is currently before the SDNY Court for enforcement under the New York Convention.

Current Legal Status (Dec 2025)

October 2024: Final Award

ICC Tribunal issues Final Award favoring Qatar Pharma.

Sept 2025: Removal to Federal Court

Saudi Arabia removes the case from NY State Court to US District Court (SDNY).

December 5, 2025: Petitioners' Reply

Qatar Pharma files opposition to Saudi Arabia's motion to dismiss. Arguments focus on waiver of Sovereign Immunity via the investment treaty.

Regulatory Conclusion

Most Likely Outcome: Confirmation of Award.

U.S. courts heavily favor the enforcement of international arbitration awards. While Saudi Arabia has raised defenses regarding sovereign immunity and venue, the "arbitration exception" to the FSIA typically applies when a state has signed an investment treaty agreeing to arbitrate.

Impact on Pharma: This case highlights the importance of Investment Treaties (ISDS) in protecting pharmaceutical assets abroad against political risks like blockades or regulatory expropriation.
